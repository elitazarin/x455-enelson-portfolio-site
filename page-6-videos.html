RX MGMT Paper Prototyping

Before creating a digital prototype, I created a low fidelity paper prototype to try to find usability and design issues. Keeping everything black and white and fairly rudimentary allowed users to feel more comfortable giving honest feedback that could result in a redesign. Please excuse the slight difficulties with moving the screens through my handmade cardboard iPhone.

RxMGMT Digital Prototype Walkthrough

After testing out my paper prototype, it was time to move it into the digital realm. There weren't any major usability issues uncovered via the paper prototyping session, so it was primarily a matter of building out and refining my wireframes to get them ready for additional testing.

RxMGMT Case Study Presentation

After conducting user testing and gathering my findings, it was time to synthesize what I'd created and learned. I learned a lot over the course of this project. I started it because I saw how difficult it was for a friend of mine to get vital seizure medications, and I'm happy to say they were pretty pleased with the result. Of course, there is plenty of room for improvement. I did a mockup of one page incorporating the feedback and a much more aesthetically pleasing color scheme you'll see at the end of the video.
